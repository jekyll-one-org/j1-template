When discussing **Words Per Minute (WPM)**, it is important to distinguish
between **speaking**, **reading**, and **typing**, as each has different
benchmarks across languages.

A fascinating linguistic finding is that while some languages are spoken
"faster" in terms of syllables, almost all human languages convey information
at roughly the same rate—about **39 bits per second**.

### 1. Speaking Rate

English is often used as the "baseline" for speaking speed. While Spanish
and Italian *sound* faster, they often use more words or syllables to
convey the same idea that English can express more densely.

| Language | Estimated WPM (Conversational) | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **US English** | **150 – 160** | Generally considered a "medium-density" language. |
| **Spanish** | **160 – 210** | High syllable rate; often cited as one of the fastest European languages. |
| **French** | **150 – 190** | Slightly faster than English; characterized by a high number of syllables per second. |
| **Italian** | **160 – 180** | Similar to Spanish in its rapid syllabic flow. |
| **German** | **130 – 150** | Slightly slower WPM due to complex, compound words and grammar. |


### 2. Silent Reading Rate

For most European languages using the Latin alphabet, the reading speed is remarkably consistent, though word length plays a major role.

* **US English (Average):** **238 WPM** for non-fiction; **260 WPM** for fiction.
* **European Languages:** Generally fall within the **200 – 300 WPM** range for native speakers.
* **The "Word Length" Factor:** In languages like German or Finnish, the WPM might appear lower because a single word (e.g., *Rindfleischetikettierungsüberwachungsaufgabenübertragungsgesetz*) carries the weight of an entire English phrase. However, the **conceptual processing speed** remains nearly identical.


### 3. Typing Speed

Typing WPM is less about the language and more about the **keyboard layout** and **orthography** (accents and special characters).

* **English:** The average typist hits **40 WPM**, while professionals reach **70–90 WPM**. Because English lacks accents, it is often the "cleanest" to type.
* **Spanish/French/Italian:** Native speakers often type at similar rates to English speakers, but WPM can drop slightly (5–10%) for non-natives because of the need to use "dead keys" for accents (e.g., `´` + `e` = `é`).
* **German:** Typing speed can be slightly slower due to the frequent use of the `Shift` key (all nouns are capitalized) and the use of umlauts (`ä`, `ö`, `ü`).

### Key Takeaway

If you are planning a speech or a presentation:

> **Aim for 130–150 WPM** regardless of the language. This is the "sweet spot" where audiences can comfortably process information without feeling rushed.
