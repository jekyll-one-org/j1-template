[role="mt-5"]
=== Native player

The examples below use only the *built-in HTML5 audio engine* of
your browser. The audio file (e.g. an MP3) is loaded directly from the
web server. No external service is involved. This is the simplest and
most reliable way to add audio to a J1 page. Use a *native* player when
you host the audio file yourself.

[role="mt-4"]
==== Mini player

In the context of complex components on a web page, like an audio player,
a mini player refers to a minimized version of full players. It typically
offers basic playback controls, such as pause, play, and
volume adjustment, while taking up less screen space.

.Emancipator · From Dusk To Dawn
amplitude::emancipator_mini[role="mb-4"]

Mini players are beneficial when you want to listen to audio in the
background without dedicating the entire screen to the player. They
are commonly found on music streaming services, podcast platforms, and
websites with embedded audio content.

[role="mt-4"]
==== Compact player

The design of a *Compact player* is an efficient approach that involves
condensing the player controls and display elements into a smaller area.
It allows the audio player to fit neatly within the web page's layout without
overwhelming or dominating the content around it.

.Emancipator · From Dusk To Dawn
amplitude::emancipator_compact[role="mb-4"]

The player aims to balance functionality and space efficiency, ensuring users
can easily access and control the audio playback without sacrificing too much
screen space or placing  multiple players side-by-side.

[role="mt-4"]
==== Large player

The *Large player* is the full-featured Amplitude player. It shows the
album cover at full size, displays a scrollable playlist next to the
controls, and offers every button (play, pause, previous, next,
shuffle, repeat, skip, volume) directly on the page. Use it whenever
the player is the *main content* of a page or section.

.Emancipator · From Dusk To Dawn
amplitude::emancipator_large[role="mb-4"]

The large design provides the full functionality of an audio player to ensure
users can easily control audio playback. The player behaves similarly to a
compact player, but all controls are displayed in a single window; there is
no need to open other widgets.


[role="mt-5"]
=== Player over YouTube

The examples below show how to play the *audio* track of a *YouTube
video* through an Amplitude player. The J1 Template provides a small
plugin called *ytp* that extends the standard Amplitude API so it can
read YouTube as if it were a normal audio file. Only the *audio* part
is played -- the YouTube video itself is hidden.

[role="mt-4 mb-5"]
[NOTE]
====
The YouTube plugin is only available inside the J1 Template under the
name *ytp*. At the moment it works *only* with the *Large* player.

Behind the scenes, the plugin loads a hidden YouTube `<iframe>` player
and forwards every button click (play, pause, next, ...) to it. From
your point of view, the player looks and feels exactly like a normal
audio player with a playlist -- you do not have to learn the YouTube
API to use it.
====

Extended the ytp introduction by two facts verified in the sources:

  * privacy: the hidden YT iframe is served from the privacy-enhanced
    host youtube-nocookie.com when `privacy_enhanced` is enabled
    (default: true), see ytp.js `privacyEnhancedHost`;
  * partial playback: the `start` and `end` timestamps of a title
    (amplitude_media.yml) are evaluated by the plugin, see ytp.js
    `timestamp2seconds` conversion of `activeSong.start|end`.
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
////

Two details of the plugin are worth knowing:

* *Privacy:* by default, the hidden YouTube player is loaded from the
  privacy-enhanced host `youtube-nocookie.com`. This host does not
  place tracking cookies before a video is played. You can control
  this per player with the setting `privacy_enhanced` in
  `amplitude_control.yml`.
* *Partial playback:* for every YouTube title you can set the
  timestamps `start` and `end` in `amplitude_media.yml` to play only
  a part of the video -- for example to skip an intro or cut off an
  outro.

[role="mt-4"]
==== Olivia Dean

Olivia Dean is a British soul and pop singer from London. Her warm
voice and honest songs about love and everyday life made her one of
the most popular young British artists of the last years. The playlist
below plays titles of her album *The Art Of Loving* directly from
YouTube -- only the audio track is heard, the video stays hidden.

.Olivia Dean · The Art Of Loving · YouTube
amplitude::olivia_dean_yt_large[role="mb-4"]

The player above is defined in `amplitude_control.yml` as follows:

[source, yaml]
----
    - player:
      enabled:                          true
      id:                               olivia_dean_yt_large
      type:                             large
      source:                           video
      privacy_enhanced:                 true
      playlist:
        name:                           olivia_dean_yt
      plugin_manager:
        enabled:                        true
        plugins:                        [ ytp ]
----

The playlist `olivia_dean_yt` is defined in `amplitude_media.yml`. Note
the YouTube-specific values: the album `audio_base` is `//youtube.com`,
every title's `audio` value is the watch path of the video (for example
`watch?v=VI0NDsh2b8k`), and the `start` and `end` timestamps limit the
playback of each title.
